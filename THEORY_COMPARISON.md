# Theory Comparison Matrix

## Core Assumptions

| Theory | Core Mechanism | Group Formation | Dynamics | Mathematics |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|-----------|-------------|
| **CFT** | Threshold-based consensus | Binary (in/out) | Equilibrium seeking | Graph theory, combinatorics |
| **GFT** | Gradient following | Continuous clustering | Always evolving | Differential equations |
| **QST** | Quantum superposition | Probabilistic | Measurement-dependent | Quantum mechanics |
| **ICT** | Information flow | Communication-limited | Cascade dynamics | Information theory |
| **TST** | Energy minimization | Statistical | Temperature-dependent | Statistical mechanics |

## Key Differentiators

### State Space
- **CFT**: Discrete partitions
- **GFT**: Continuous positions in trait space
- **QST**: Superposition of states
- **ICT**: Network with information states
- **TST**: Microstates with energies

### Time Evolution  
- **CFT**: Rapid convergence to stable equilibrium
- **GFT**: Smooth trajectories following gradients
- **QST**: Unitary evolution until measurement
- **ICT**: Punctuated by information events
- **TST**: Stochastic with temperature-dependent rates

### Predictions

| Scenario | CFT | GFT | QST | ICT | TST |
|----------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Small group (n<10) | Deterministic clusters | Smooth clustering | Measurement-dependent | Bandwidth-limited | Fluctuating |
| Large group (n>100) | Few large blocks | Many local clusters | Decoherence → classical | Hierarchical structure | Phase transitions |
| Dynamic environment | Rare reorganization | Continuous adaptation | Constant superposition | Information cascades | Temperature tracking |
| Observer effects | None | None | Fundamental | Indirect (info spread) | None |
| Long-range effects | None | Decay with distance | Entanglement | Network-dependent | Mean field |

## Computational Complexity

| Theory | Time Complexity | Space Complexity | Parallelizable |
|--------|----------------|------------------|----------------|
| **CFT** | O(2^n) worst case | O(n²) | Partially |
| **GFT** | O(n²) per timestep | O(n) | Highly |
| **QST** | O(2^n) full quantum | O(2^n) | Limited |
| **ICT** | O(n·m) m=edges | O(n+m) | Moderately |
| **TST** | O(n²) Monte Carlo | O(n) | Highly |

## Best Use Cases

### CFT (Consensus-Fracture Theory)
✅ **Best for:**
- Ideological groupings with clear boundaries
- Stable organizational structures  
- Binary coalition decisions
- Small to medium groups (n < 50)

❌ **Not ideal for:**
- Fluid social dynamics
- Large-scale systems
- Continuous opinion spaces

### GFT (Gradient Field Theory)
✅ **Best for:**
- Continuous trait/opinion spaces
- Geographic or spatial grouping
- Smooth social dynamics
- Systems with natural metrics

❌ **Not ideal for:**
- Binary decisions
- Discrete categories
- Sudden transitions

### QST (Quantum Social Theory)
✅ **Best for:**
- Small groups with strong measurement effects
- Uncertain/ambiguous situations
- Entangled relationships
- Observer-dependent phenomena

❌ **Not ideal for:**
- Large systems (decoherence)
- Classical deterministic scenarios
- Long-time predictions

### ICT (Information Cascade Theory)
✅ **Best for:**
- Information-driven communities
- Social media dynamics
- Expertise-based grouping
- Communication-constrained systems

❌ **Not ideal for:**
- Perfect information scenarios
- Non-communicating agents
- Static relationships

### TST (Thermodynamic Social Theory)
✅ **Best for:**
- Large statistical systems
- Phase transitions (revolutions, crashes)
- Long-term equilibrium behavior
- Systems with clear "energy" analogues

❌ **Not ideal for:**
- Small groups
- Detailed individual predictions
- Non-equilibrium dynamics

## Hybrid Approaches

### Natural Combinations
1. **CFT + GFT**: Discrete groups with continuous internal dynamics
2. **ICT + TST**: Information temperature and phase transitions
3. **QST + GFT**: Quantum effects in continuous spaces
4. **CFT + ICT**: Threshold formation with information constraints

### Multi-Scale Integration
- **Micro**: QST for individual decisions
- **Meso**: CFT for group formation
- **Macro**: TST for population statistics

## Empirical Distinguishability

### Key Experiments to Differentiate
1. **Formation Speed**: CFT (fast) vs GFT (gradual)
2. **Boundary Sharpness**: CFT (sharp) vs GFT (fuzzy)
3. **Observer Effects**: QST (strong) vs others (none)
4. **Information Dependence**: ICT (critical) vs CFT (secondary)
5. **Size Scaling**: TST (phase transitions) vs CFT (combintorial limits)

### Measurable Signatures
| Theory | Unique Signature |
|--------|-----------------|
| **CFT** | Step-function group membership |
| **GFT** | Continuous drift in trait space |
| **QST** | Measurement-induced transitions |
| **ICT** | Information cascade avalanches |
| **TST** | Critical phenomena at transitions |

## Selection Guide

```
Is group membership binary?
├─ Yes → Is it threshold-based?
│  ├─ Yes → CFT
│  └─ No → Is measurement important?
│     ├─ Yes → QST
│     └─ No → ICT
└─ No → Are dynamics continuous?
   ├─ Yes → GFT  
   └─ No → Is system large?
      ├─ Yes → TST
      └─ No → Hybrid approach
```

This comparison matrix helps select appropriate theories and design discriminating experiments.
